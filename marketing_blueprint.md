# Yeled.ai — Full Marketing Blueprint & Monetization Plan

## 0) CMO Executive Lens

Yeled.ai should be positioned as the **category-defining AI planning and parent-communication copilot for Jewish early-years education**—not generic edtech and not nursery admin software.

Core CMO objective over the next 24 months:
1. Own mindshare in Jewish nursery leadership circles in the UK/US/Israel diaspora segments.
2. Prove hard ROI (time saved, communication quality, staff confidence).
3. Build a repeatable revenue engine from pilots → paid subscriptions → network expansion.

---

## 1) Strategic Positioning

## Category
- **AI workflow SaaS for Jewish early years settings**.

## Positioning statement
- “Yeled.ai helps Jewish nurseries save planning time, improve parent communication, and raise educational consistency with culturally aligned AI tools staff actually use.”

## What we are (and are not)
- Are: planning + parent insights workflow platform.
- Are not: billing, attendance, full MIS replacement.

## Messaging pillars
1. **Time Back for Educators** — reduce planning/admin burden.
2. **Jewish Context Native** — parsha, yom tov, mitzvot, Hebrew vocabulary, and setting-specific terminology.
3. **Higher-Quality Communication** — clearer, kinder, development-aware parent updates.
4. **Manager Control & Consistency** — settings and quality standards across team outputs.

## 2) ICP, Segmentation, and TAM Logic

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Primary:
- Jewish nursery / early years settings (10–80 children, 4–25 staff members).
- Decision-maker: nursery manager, headteacher, owner/director.
- Buying trigger: staff stretched, inconsistent planning, parent comms quality concerns.

Secondary:
- Multi-site groups and umbrella organizations (federations, school networks).

## Segments to prioritize (Go-to-market order)
1. **Innovator managers** (high pain + high adoption willingness).
2. **Word-of-mouth clusters** (same city/community leadership circles).
3. **Network buyers** (multi-site operators).

## Persona pain map
- Manager pain: uneven output quality, staff development gaps, limited time for mentoring.
- Staff pain: blank-page anxiety, time pressure, uncertainty in wording to parents.
- Parent pain (indirect): variable communication clarity and confidence in nursery quality.

## 7) Monetization Model (Primary + Secondary)

## Primary model: SaaS subscription (per nursery, tiered)

### Proposed packaging
**Starter** (small teams)
- Core planning + parent insights
- Up to X staff seats
- Basic history and exports

**Professional** (default target)
- Higher usage limits
- Advanced templates/settings
- Manager analytics dashboard
- Priority support

**Network** (multi-site)
- Multi-site controls
- Central reporting
- Dedicated success lead
- Contracted annual terms

## Pricing logic
- Anchor price to quantified time savings (hours saved × staff hourly cost).
- Keep starter affordable to reduce initial adoption friction.
- Use annual plans for discount + cash flow predictability.

Illustrative pricing framework (to validate in pilots):
- Starter: $99–$149/month
- Professional: $199–$399/month
- Network: custom ($8k–$50k+ ARR depending on site count)

## Secondary monetization (Year 2+)
1. **Premium content packs** (holiday/parsha packs, specialist pedagogy bundles).
2. **Training & certification** (AI-in-practice for early years teams).
3. **Implementation services** for multi-site groups.
4. **API/integration add-ons** (when integration roadmap matures).
